Market Reaction to ESG Rating Changes

Event Study with Abnormal Return Analysis

Languages: Python, R & Stata Category: Financial Econometrics
Research Question Do stock prices react significantly when a company's ESG rating is upgraded or downgraded?
Methodology

Classical event study methodology. The market model estimates expected returns in a pre-event window, then abnormal returns (AR) and cumulative abnormal returns (CAR) are computed in the event window. Statistical significance is assessed via cross-sectional t-tests.

Data Collection & Sources
Source: Simulated events modelled on MSCI ESG rating change announcements
Design: 50 upgrade events, 50 downgrade events
Windows: Estimation [-250, -30], Event [-5, +10]
Benchmark: Market model with S&P 500 as market proxy
Key Methods
Market Model
R_i = α + β·R_m + ε — estimated over [-250,-30] to establish 'normal' expected returns
Abnormal Returns
AR_t = R_actual − R_expected — the difference between what happened and what the model predicted
CAR Analysis
Cumulative sum of ARs over the event window — captures the total stock price impact of the rating change
Key Findings
1
ESG upgrades generate a small positive CAR of +0.8% over [-1, +3], statistically significant at the 10% level
2
ESG downgrades produce a larger negative CAR of −1.4% over the same window, significant at the 5% level — asymmetric market response
3
Most of the price adjustment occurs within 2 days, consistent with semi-strong market efficiency
